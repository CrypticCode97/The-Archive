2. Categories of data we process

Depending on usage, we may process the following categories:

  • Discord identifiers, server identifiers, and channel metadata required to provide bot functions.
  • Uploaded document files (currently PDF) and extracted text used for retrieval and citation.
  • Query text and related request metadata to deliver responses and maintain abuse protections.
  • Usage and quota counters at server level.
  • Plan and billing metadata (for example subscription status and provider reference IDs) when paid features are enabled.
  • Operational logs necessary for reliability, security, and incident response.

3. Legal bases (GDPR-oriented)

Where GDPR applies, processing is based on one or more of:

  • Contract necessity under Article 6(1)(b) to provide the requested service.
  • Legitimate interests under Article 6(1)(f) for service security, abuse prevention, and reliability.
  • Legal obligations under Article 6(1)(c) where recordkeeping or compliance duties apply.
  • Consent under Article 6(1)(a) where consent is specifically requested.

4. How we use personal data

  • Provide and maintain Archivist features, including document indexing and query responses.
  • Enforce configured plan limits, retention settings, and moderation controls.
  • Monitor service health, detect abuse, and protect infrastructure integrity.
  • Handle support, legal inquiries, and data rights requests.

5. Sharing and processors

We do not sell personal data. We may share limited data with processors who support service delivery, including:

  • Discord platform infrastructure for bot operations.
  • Hosting and infrastructure providers for data storage and compute.
  • Payment providers for subscription lifecycle handling, if paid plans are used.

Processor access is limited to what is required for their role and is governed by contractual safeguards.

6. Data retention

We retain data only as long as needed for service operation and legal obligations.

  • Document retention is configurable by server administrators, typically from 48 hours up to 1 year.
  • Expired documents may be automatically deleted according to configured retention policy.
  • Logs and billing metadata are retained for operational integrity, dispute handling, and compliance needs.
